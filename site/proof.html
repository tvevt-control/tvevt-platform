Why a TVEVT record can be trusted.

A TVEVT record is not just a text page. It is a fixed proof artifact with a timestamp, a hash, and a verification surface.

This page explains what a sealed record means, what it proves, and what it does not claim.

Seal a statement Open verification page

What happens when you seal a statement

The system takes your text and converts it into a record that can be checked later.

1

Seal

The exact statement text is submitted as a payload. At this point, the system creates a fixed record basis.

2

Proof

The record receives an ID, timestamp, and hash. These fields make the record referenceable and checkable.

3

Verify

Anyone with the record ID or link can open the verification page and inspect the proof fields tied to that statement.

What the proof is actually saying

A TVEVT record makes a narrow and important claim.

This text was sealed as a record. The system keeps a reference point for the statement in the form shown at the time of sealing.
This record has proof fields. Record ID, timestamp, current hash, previous hash, and sequence create a verifiable surface.
This is not “trust me.” The point is not opinion. The point is that the record can be inspected and referenced later.

What you can verify

On the record page, a user can inspect the key fields that define the proof surface. 

{
  "id": "TVE-123ABC",
  "timestamp": "2026-04-20T12:34:56Z",
  "sequence": 14,
  "prev_hash": "7f83b1657ff1fc53b92dc18148a1d65dfa135014...",
  "hash": "e3b0c44298fc1c149afbf4c8996fb92427ae41e4...",
  "author_id": "example-source",
  "content": {
    "text": "This is the sealed statement.",
    "type": "text/plain"
  },
  "metadata": {}
}

If the sealed payload changes, the hash changes too. That is why the proof surface matters.

What TVEVT proof does not claim

Trust grows when claims stay precise.

It does not claim legal notarization. A TVEVT record is a proof artifact, not a replacement for every formal legal process.
It does not prove truth of content. It proves that a specific statement was sealed in a specific record context.
It does not replace judgment. It provides reference, integrity, and traceability — not interpretation for the viewer.

Why this still matters

Even a narrow proof claim is powerful in the real world.

It reduces ambiguity. Instead of arguing from memory, people can point to a specific record.
It creates a stable reference point. The record becomes the canonical place to inspect what was sealed.
It gives digital statements weight. A link with proof fields carries more credibility than an ordinary plain-text message.
