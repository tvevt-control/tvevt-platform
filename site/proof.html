Why a sealed signal can be trusted.

TVEVT gives each sealed signal a fixed verification artifact: timestamped, hashed, stored, and accessible by link.

Seal a SignalVerify a Record

What happens when a signal is sealed

The system transforms a statement into a stable record that can be referenced later. The record is not an editable page. It is a verification surface.

SignalThe user enters the exact content of the statement.
SealThe system creates a record ID, timestamp, and cryptographic hash.
VerifyThe record can be opened later as a proof artifact by verification link.

What the proof shows

TimestampThe moment the signal was sealed.
HashA digital fingerprint of the sealed content.
Record IDA stable reference used to open and verify the artifact.

What it does not claim

Not factual truthIt proves that this specific statement was sealed as a record.
Not legal notarizationIt creates a digital proof artifact, not a universal legal substitute.
Not an editorThe verification page inspects the record. It does not alter it.
